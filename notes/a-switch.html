a switch

Clearly, we should be able to talk about a switch.

A switch is a thing that

  • can be in one of two states and
  • that responds to messages asking that its state be changed.

What do I mean by “talk about?”

I should be able to assert that something is a switch, or has a switch (has something that is a switch).

We should be able to say that a switch is “connected to” something, where that something is a cell. Where a cell is something that can reflect different states at different times.

But we're not talking about cells that hold values; we're talking about cells that hold monotonically-increasing information about values.

How does this apply to the idea of a switch?

Whereas we would normally think of a switch as being in some state at a given time (on or off) simply because “that's the state it's in,” the idea here is that, while it might appear that the state of the switch can be directly changed, the switch's state is really derivative of any number of factors, only one of which is our manipulation. This switch in this case represents the truth of some proposition on which other knowledge weighs.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reason_maintenance

Reason maintenance is a knowledge representation approach to efficient handling of inferred information that is explicitly stored. Reason maintenance distinguishes between base facts, which can be defeated, and derived facts. As such it differs from belief revision which, in its basic form, assumes that all facts are equally important.
